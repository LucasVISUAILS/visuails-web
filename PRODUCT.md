# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Solo and small direct-to-consumer e-commerce founders — people running their own brand with no in-house creative team, a real catalog to shoot, and a budget that won't stretch to a studio. Their context is practical and time-poor: they'd rather grow the business than schedule, brief, and pay for another photo shoot. The job to be done is getting publish-ready product visuals — for the shop, marketplaces, ads, and social — that look like a bigger, more established brand than they can currently afford to be. Success is a founder who sends one photo, gets a set back the same day, publishes it everywhere, and starts to look coherent across every SKU.

## Product Purpose

VISUAILS is a product-visual studio. A customer sends a single product photo and receives a finished, publish-ready set of catalog shots, styled lifestyle scenes, and short product videos — delivered in about 24 hours, every image inspected by a person before it ships, from €19 per visual. It exists because studio-grade visual production is slow, expensive, and logistically heavy for small brands, while raw AI tools are fast and cheap but inconsistent and untrusted. VISUAILS sits deliberately between the two: the speed and price of automation, the consistency and human judgment of a studio. Success is a small brand that looks like a considered, campaign-grade one — without ever booking a shoot.

## Positioning

One photo in, a full campaign-grade set out, in about 24 hours. Every screen reinforces that the customer's entire contribution is a single clear photo, and that what comes back is finished, consistent, and ready to publish — no studio, no scheduling, no shoots.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: **Chat on WhatsApp** — the fastest, most human route to a first order. Secondary CTA: **Claim the free test sample** (one free visual made from the visitor's own product photo) — the proof-first fallback for visitors not ready to open a conversation.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: **"Look like the brand you're becoming."**
- Belief ladder — what a visitor must come to believe, in order, before acting: (1) these visuals are genuinely good enough to publish; (2) they'll stay consistent across my whole catalog, so I read as one brand, not a patchwork of shoots; (3) it's genuinely easy and fast — I just send one photo; (4) it's real quality, checked by a person, not raw AI output; (5) it's affordable and honest — from €19, the price is on the page, no subscription or lock-in.
- Proof on hand: no testimonials, case studies, or client logos yet — the brand deliberately runs **no fabricated social proof**. Current proof is verifiable value claims only (roughly 10× cheaper than a traditional shoot, ~24–48h delivery, 100% human-checked, from €19, three revision rounds included) and the free test sample that lets a visitor judge the work on their own product. Add real testimonials and case studies once clients exist.

## Brand Personality

Confident, calm, minimal — a studio that speaks as a quiet expert rather than a salesperson. The voice is assured and understated: it states what it does plainly, lets the work and the transformation carry the argument, and never oversells. Editorial restraint is the register — big serif display with italic emphasis, generous quiet, and a single warm accent spent only on the action. The feeling to evoke is trust and quiet ambition: the sense that working with VISUAILS is how a small brand grows into a bigger-looking one.

## Anti-references

Explicitly not any of these: the cheap AI-generator / gig-marketplace look (Fiverr-grade "AI headshot generator" energy that would undercut the promise of hand-checked quality); the generic SaaS / tech-startup landing page (gradient blobs, Inter-everywhere, endless feature grids — the default AI aesthetic); the busy discount e-commerce look (coupon banners, red sale badges, cluttered marketplace clutter that reads as cheap); and the bland corporate stock-photo agency site (smiling-team stock imagery, safe corporate blue). The brand's credibility depends on looking like a real studio, not like the automated tools it competes against.

## Design Principles

Show, don't tell — the transformation (before/after, free sample, real work) makes the argument; copy stays quiet. Restraint is the brand — one accent, spent only on the action; whitespace and typography carry weight, not decoration. Consistency is the product — the same light, framing, and faces across every visual is both what VISUAILS sells and how the site itself must behave. Honest by default — real, verifiable claims and transparent pricing; never fabricated proof or manufactured urgency. Effortless for the customer — every screen reinforces that their whole job is sending one photo.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text holds ≥4.5:1 contrast against the warm near-black canvas (and large/display text ≥3:1) — verify muted ink tokens (`--ink-2`, `--ink-3`) against surfaces rather than assuming. Fully keyboard-navigable with visible focus states (the existing `:focus-visible` coral outline). Motion honors `prefers-reduced-motion` with crossfade/instant fallbacks — already scaffolded in the reset. The coral accent must never be the sole carrier of meaning; pair it with text or shape for color-blind users.
